## Context

Foresters maintain V1 Merkle trees by dequeuing nullifiers and addresses, but they currently pay Solana transaction fees out of pocket with no reimbursement. The `network_fee` field in tree metadata (described as "Tip for maintaining the account") exists for exactly this purpose but is never paid out. Users already pay `network_fee` (5000 lamports for state, 10000 for address) per operation, and these fees accumulate in the tree/queue accounts. This plan adds the actual lamport transfer from queue accounts to the forester when they perform V1 operations on trees with `network_fee > 0`.

## Scope

- Only V1 (non-batched) tree instructions in `account-compression`
- Only trees where `network_fee > 0` (light forester trees)
- Two instructions: `nullify_leaves` and `update_address_merkle_tree`
- Not V1 rollover (already reimburses rent; rollover is a one-time event)
- Not V2 batched operations (out of scope)

## Design

### How it works

1. Add a `fee_payer` account to V1 `NullifyLeaves` and `UpdateAddressMerkleTree` instruction structs
2. After the tree operation, read `network_fee` from tree metadata
3. If `network_fee > 0`, transfer `network_fee` lamports from the queue account to `fee_payer`
4. Registry CPI wrappers pass the forester's `authority` (wallet) as `fee_payer`

### Why queue accounts?
Network fees accumulate in queue accounts when users insert items. When the forester dequeues items, the fee is paid back from the same queue. This is economically clean: fees in, reimbursement out.

### Reimbursement amount
- `nullify_leaves`: `merkle_tree.metadata.rollover_metadata.network_fee` per nullify (currently 5000 lamports)
- `update_address_merkle_tree`: `merkle_tree.metadata.rollover_metadata.network_fee` per update (currently 10000 lamports)
